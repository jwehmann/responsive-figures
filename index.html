Example 4: LAB About Us Page

Our Principles

  • Place-based: Tailored support to the unique geographic, cultural, social, and economic context of a community.
  • Evidence-based: Anchored in the latest research and data.
  • Impact-focused: Designed to produce measurable, equitable outcomes.
  • Human-centered: Focused on the experiences, needs, and perspectives of the people involved.

Our Expertise

  • Adult learning and curriculum design
  • Systems and design thinking
  • Facilitating group decision-making and learning
  • Strategic advising
  • Policy and research
  • Community and stakeholder engagement
  • Training and coaching

Our Methods

  • Develop and lead peer learning and communities of practice to facilitate shared problem-solving and help local leaders develop and implement proven strategies.
  • Provide customized 1-on-1 technical assistance and coaching that builds internal expertise and strengthens local capacity.
  • Guide organizations to clarify their impact through developing/ refining a theory of change and/or logic model.
  • Convene funders, policymakers, and practitioners to spark collaboration around emerging challenges and effective solutions.
  • Deliver group-based interactive skill-building training through workshops, bootcamps, and eLearning modules that emphasize hands-on practice and real-world application
  • Develop toolkits, playbooks, guides that codify best practices and ensure learning can be shared and scaled.

Our Outcomes

  • Partners use data and evidence to inform decisions.
  • Communities engage meaningfully in shaping solutions.
  • Organizations build sustainable, equity-driven practices.
  • Local leaders connect with peers and experts to innovate.
  • Systems change is guided by measurable impact and shared learning.

How We Work

No matter the focus of our training and technical assistance, we help partners reach five goals:



  • Partners use data to drive decisions

  • Communities shape solutions that reflect their needs

  • Organizations build sustainable, equity-driven practices

  • Leaders connect and innovate together

  • Measurable outcomes guide system-level changes

We help partners achieve these results by grounding our work in four core principles.


  • Place-based:
    Tailored support to the unique context of a community.

  • Evidence-based:
    Anchored in the latest research and data.

  • Impact-focused:
    Designed to produce measurable outcomes that improve lives.

  • Human-centered:
    Focused on experiences, needs, and perspectives of the people involved.

What We Offer

Peer learning and communities of practice. We facilitate shared problem-solving among local leaders, practitioners, and experts.

In our Mobility Action Learning Network, 26 communities across the US came together to advance upward mobility in their areas. Focused on building coalitions for system-level change, teams from Alexandria, Virginia; Dallas; and New Orleans expanded cross-sector partnerships that generated new collaborative strategies for addressing poverty and systemic inequities.

Customized technical assistance and coaching. We create technical assistance and coaching support that build partners’ internal capacity, strengthen their program planning and implementation, and lead to system-level change.

As part of the Community Violence Intervention Research and Evaluation Technical Assistance Center, we delivered workshops that helped more than 70 grantees build the knowledge and skills to measure and evaluate programmatic efforts. With coaching support from Urban experts after the workshops, grantees developed theories of change, built their capacity to effectively measure outcomes and align strategies with local contexts, and fostered trust with others working in community violence intervention.

Example 1: Used in the Exploring Spatial Gaps in Access to Low-Wage Jobs by Race and Ethnicity" digital factsheet.

We aggregate this access measure to the city and metro level for each race and ethnicity group using a weighted average by race/ethnicity at the census tract level. Overall, this measure reflects public transit connectivity, housing availability, and the proximity of job opportunities for communities. We display this measure as the gap in access, which is the percentage difference in access to jobs between people of color and people who are non-Hispanic white.

Four steps in creating the access measure

Calculating Gaps in Spatial Access to Jobs

Calculate transit commute time between all census tracts within each metropolitan area

Calculate competition-adjusted access to jobs via transit for each census tract

Calculate each locality and metropolitan area's average access to jobs by race/ethnicity each year from 2007 to 2019

Calculate the percentage difference in access to jobs between people of color and people who are white
